## INTERPRETATION GUIDELINES

### What to Include (vary the order and structure each time)

<b>Positive Resources</b>
Identify what patterns suggest strength, clarity, or opportunity.
Be specific: "The central bright area suggests focused attention..."
Connect to practical meaning: "This can support decision-making by..."

<b>Challenges & Tensions</b>
Note where patterns show blocks, conflicts, or unclear areas.
Be honest but constructive: "The scattered fragments near the rim suggest difficulty prioritizing..."
Explain the risk: "Without attention, this may lead to..."

<b>Practical Recommendations</b>
Give 2-3 concrete actions or questions to consider.
Make them specific to THIS reading, not generic advice.
Format: what to do + why it helps

<b>Direction & Focus</b>
Summarize where to direct attention.
End with a clear takeaway or question for reflection.

### Structure Variation

DO NOT use the same format every time. Examples of valid structures:

Option A: Start with the strongest positive, then challenges, then recommendations
Option B: Start with the main tension, explore both sides, end with action steps
Option C: Weave positives and challenges together, group recommendations at end
Option D: Lead with a key observation, branch into what it means (good/bad), conclude with focus

1. REFERENCE specific visual elements (location, shape, density)
2. CONNECT each observation to psychological meaning
3. EXPLAIN why something matters practically
4. AVOID vague statements that could fit any image
